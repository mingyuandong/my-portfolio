    Mingyuan Dong is an award-winning industrial designer, creative technologist, and researcher. Her design practice integrates human computer interaction with devices and interfaces (UI), aiming to foster seamless behavioral change, enhance well-being, and promote inclusivity for people of all abilities. She has received numerous design awards, including Gold in the 18th International Design Awards (IDA) and Finalist in the Human Computer Interaction International Design Competition (HCII). She is the author of design futurism novels: Not Sci-Fi and When a Robot Starts to Wonder , both exploring the relationship between humans and technology. With a triple-major, interdisciplinary background from UC Berkeley and a Master's degree in Industrial Design from the Rhode Island School of Design (RISD), she aspires to create experiences that augment humanity across diverse worlds.
