    Mingyuan Dong (Ming) is an award-winning designer, creative technologist, and design researcher. She is passionate about creating tangible user interfaces (TUIs) and physical objects with embedded interactions. Her research centers on optimizing the relationship between objects and interfaces, with the design goal of fostering seamless behavioral change, enhancing well-being, and promoting inclusivity for people of all abilities. She has received numerous design awards, including Gold in the 18th International Design Awards (IDA) and Finalist in the Human Computer Interaction International Design Competition (HCII). She is the author of design futurism novels: Not Sci-Fi and When a Robot Starts to Wonder , both exploring the relationship between humans and technology. As an interdisciplinary practitioner, she completed a triple major at UC Berkeley, is currently pursuing a Masters degree in Design at the Rhode Island School of Design (RISD), and works as an HCI design researcher at Brown University. She aspires to create multi-layered experiences that augment humanity across diverse worlds.
